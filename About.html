About the Army and the Exam

Perks of Being in the Army

  • Opportunity to serve and protect the nation.
  • Competitive salary and benefits, including healthcare and housing.
  • Professional development and leadership training.
  • Travel opportunities and exposure to diverse cultures.
  • Strong sense of camaraderie and teamwork.
  • Post-service career benefits and pension plans.

How the Exam Will Be Conducted

The Army Entry Exam is designed to evaluate candidates' aptitude, physical fitness, and overall suitability for military service. The process includes the following stages:

  • Written Test: Covers general knowledge, aptitude, and basic mathematics. The test duration is 2 hours.
  • Physical Fitness Test: Includes tasks such as running, push-ups, and other exercises to assess physical readiness.
  • Medical Examination: Ensures candidates meet the required health standards.
  • Personal Interview: Conducted to evaluate the candidate's motivation, communication skills, and suitability for the role.

Detailed instructions and schedules will be provided after the application process is complete.
